Given all data is stored in digital computers and [Encoded#1.3.6.1.4.1.33097.5.5](Encoding.md) as ones and zeros, this data can be [Re-Encoded#1.3.6.1.4.1.33097.5.5](Encoding.md) as either a single integer or integer [Array# 1.3.6.1.4.1.33097.1.1.0](../data_structures/Arrays.md) a form of [ConstantHashCodeChaining#1.3.6.1.4.1.33097.1.0.4.3](../data_structures/ConsistentHashCodeChains.md).  This provides us with a manor of encoding all data with a common [ConsistentHashCode#1.3.6.1.4.1.33097.1.0.4.1](../data_structures/ConsistentHashCodes.md) or [ConstantHashCodeChain#1.3.6.1.4.1.33097.1.0.4.3](../data_structures/ConsistentHashCodeChains.md).  This style of [Hashing#1.3.6.1.4.1.33097.1.0.4.0](../data_structures/HashCodes.md) also will likely provide additional benefits like ordering (i.e. alphabetization) as authors of systems like ASCII and UTF-8 [Encoded#1.3.6.1.4.1.33097.5.5](Encoding.md) the data sequentially.
